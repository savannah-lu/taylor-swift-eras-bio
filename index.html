Taylor Swift (debut)

After signing with the then-start-up label Big Machine Records, Taylor released her first album, a self-titled country record, with notable hits such as Tim McGraw, Our Song, and Teardrops On My Guitar. Taylor aimed to bring a younger audience to country music, as opposed to the middle-aged demographic label executives were looking for.

Fearless (Taylor's Version)

Fearless is Taylor's second studio album, originally released on November 11, 2008, with notable hits such as "You Belong With Me", "Love Story", and "White Horse". In 2021, Taylor re-recorded and released "Fearless (Taylor's Version)", featuring 26 songs, including: all 19 tracks from the original deluxe version, 6 previously unreleased tracks, and the 2010 soundtrack single "Today Was A Fairytale".

Speak Now

Speak Now is Taylor's last album that is primarily country, and is one she wrote entirely herself. On the album's 17 tracks, she reflects on past relationships ("Back to December", "Dear John") and experiences ("Innocent"), the woes of growing older ("Never Grow Up"), as well as imagined scenarios she'd always been intrigued by ("Speak Now"). As of writing, it is rumored to be the next re-recorded album to be released.

Red (Taylor's Version)

Released in October 2012, Red bridged the gap between Taylor's country roots to mainstream pop. It also marked the beginning of a more mature sound from Taylor as she mourns love found and lost in tracks like "All Too Well", and she explores new themes such as the pressure and dangers of stardom. In November 2021, she re-recorded and released Red (Taylor's Version) with 9 previously unreleased tracks, including the highly anticipated 10-minute-long unabridged version of All Too Well.

1989

Inspired by 1980s synth-pop, 1989 solidified Taylor's status as a mainstream pop artist. She recruited two popular producers at the time, Max Martin and Shellback, to help push her ideas in a different direction and ensure a smooth transition to pop. The album won two Grammys and was a commercial success, but with it came more scrutiny on her private life.

reputation

reputation is Taylor's sixth and final album released under Big Machine Records. It was written and recorded during her hiatus from the spotlight, after various controversies and intense media scrutiny led her to make almost no public appearances for over a year. The album is primarily made up of two parts: one about revenge and taking back her public image, and the other of finding love during the darkest of times. She then embarked on the Reputation Stadium Tour, which currently holds the record for the highest-grossing tour in North America.

Lover

Lover, Taylor's third pop album and seventh overall, does a complete 180 from the dark tone of reputation. With the help of longtime friend and producer Jack Antonoff (and others), she incorporates more of a subdued and bubblegum pop sound in the album she describes as "a love letter to love". The album's inspiration comes from a renewed sense of freedom, in both her personal life and public persona.

folklore

Taylor continued to hop genres with the indie folk album folklore. The album was written and recorded entirely in quarantine. It was released in July 2020 during the Covid-19 pandemic, and featured a collection of stories and songs, both real and imagined. People magazine described it as "a record to match the times". The album went on to earn five Grammy nominations and won Album Of The Year.

evermore

Five months after the release of record-breaking album folklore, Taylor released its sister album, evermore. Swifties nicknamed it "the forgotten album" due to minimal promotion and little acknowledgment from Taylor after its initial release. evermore continues folklore's cozy, cottage-core theme and features collaborators Bon Iver, Haim, and the National. It tells stories of con artists falling in love, the dangers of daydreaming, and revenge plots, among others.

Midnights

Taylor returned to pop with Midnights, an album she describes as "a collection of music written in the middle of the night, a journey through terrors and sweet dreams". She revealed the track-list in the days leading up to the album's release in a TikTok series Midnights Mayhem With Me, and later revealed an extended album with seven bonus tracks, titled Midnights (3am Edition). The main themes involve the thoughts and emotions that haunt us in the dead of night. It is her most commercially successful album to date.
